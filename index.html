Snapshots from our databases

Corporate AI risk coverage by category (%)

Companies address discrimination most, existential risk least

Our analysis of ~200 major company disclosures reveals significant variation in which AI risks companies publicly address. Discrimination and privacy are covered by the majority, while concentration of power and existential risks receive minimal attention.

The average company covers only 38% of identified risk categories, suggesting substantial gaps in corporate AI risk governance.

AI risk mitigations by category (2,000+ total)

Operational processes dominate; model alignment barely mentioned

Our evidence scan of 70+ key documents found that operational process controls (testing, monitoring, validation) account for the largest share of proposed mitigations.

Notably, model alignment actions were rarely mentioned despite their fundamental importance for catastrophic and existential risks. Being developed in consultation with Stuart Russell and Yoshua Bengio.

Catastrophic risk probability under "Business as Usual" (272 experts)

14 of 24 risk domains have ≥10% catastrophic probability

Our three-round Delphi study with 272 international AI experts found that under "business as usual" assumptions, a majority of risk domains carry significant catastrophic potential. Dangerous AI capabilities (22%) and cyberattacks (21%) top the list.

Even with pragmatic mitigations, all 24 risks retain ≥5% catastrophic probability. Those most vulnerable (AI users and affected stakeholders) bear the least responsibility for mitigation, while frontier AI developers bear the most.
